How to Import Floor Scrubbers from China in 2026: A Complete Guide for International Buyers

May 23, 2026

China remains the world's largest manufacturer of industrial cleaning equipment, producing over 60% of the global supply of floor scrubbers. For distributors and large buyers outside China, importing directly from a Chinese manufacturer can save 30-50% compared to buying through local distributors. This guide walks through every step of the process.

Not all Chinese cleaning equipment factories are created equal. Here's what to look for:

Step 2: Product Selection & Customization

Chinese manufacturers typically offer three sourcing models:

  • Standard models: Ready-to-ship, best for first-time buyers. BIOCCE's BC500 and BC600 are popular entry points.
  • OEM/private label: Your brand name, colors (RAL), and packaging. MOQ typically 10-20 units.
  • ODM/custom: Modified specifications including voltage, battery type, brush configuration. MOQ varies.

Step 4: Customs Clearance & Documentation

A reliable Chinese manufacturer will provide all necessary export documents:

  • Commercial invoice (proforma and final)
  • Packing list with weights and dimensions
  • Bill of lading (sea) or airway bill (air)
  • Certificate of origin (Form E for preferential duty rates)
  • CE declaration of conformity
  • Factory test report for each machine
  • Spare parts list and user manual (available in English/Russian)

Step 5: Payment & Terms

Standard payment terms with Chinese manufacturers:

  • T/T (Telegraphic Transfer): 30% deposit, 70% before shipment — most common
  • L/C (Letter of Credit): Available for established buyers
  • Sample orders: 100% payment via PayPal or Western Union (typically $50-200 for sample evaluation)
