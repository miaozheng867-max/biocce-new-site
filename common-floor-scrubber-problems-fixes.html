Maintenance Tips

7 Common Floor Scrubber Problems and How to Fix Them

Floor scrubbers are built to handle tough daily cleaning, but even the best machines can develop issues over time. When your scrubber is not performing properly, every minute of downtime costs you money.

This troubleshooting guide covers the seven most common floor scrubber problems, what causes them, and how to fix them quickly. Most issues can be resolved in minutes without calling a technician.

1. Poor Water Pickup (Floor Still Wet After Cleaning)

Cause: Worn or damaged squeegee blades, blocked vacuum hose, dirty recovery tank filter, or incorrect squeegee angle.

Fix:

  • Check the squeegee blades for wear. Replace them if they are cracked or rounded at the edge.
  • Inspect the squeegee angle. The blade should tilt slightly backward (about 45 degrees) for optimal water collection.
  • Remove and clean the vacuum hose. Debris inside the hose restricts suction.
  • Flush the recovery tank and clean the float shut-off valve.

Prevention tip: Replace squeegee blades every 3–6 months depending on usage. BIOCCE offers replacement blade kits for all walk-behind and ride-on scrubbers.

2. Streaks or Marks Left on the Floor

Cause: Excess cleaning solution, dirty recovery tank, worn squeegee rubber, or using the wrong brush type for your floor surface.

Fix:

  • Reduce the water flow rate. More water does not mean better cleaning — it often means more streaking.
  • Use the correct detergent concentration. Too much chemical leaves residue that shows as streaks.
  • Clean or replace squeegee blades.
  • Switch to softer brushes or pads for polished or coated floors.

Prevention tip: BIOCCE walk-behind scrubbers come with adjustable water flow controls. Fine-tune the flow rate for your specific floor type to eliminate streaking.

3. Battery Drains Too Quickly

Cause: Batteries not fully charged, sulfation from incomplete charging cycles, worn-out batteries (beyond lifespan), or excessive load on the machine.

Fix:

  • Allow batteries to charge for the full recommended cycle (typically 8–10 hours for lead-acid).
  • Check water levels in lead-acid batteries. Low water levels reduce capacity.
  • Use a smart charger that prevents overcharging and maintains optimal charge cycles.
  • If batteries are over 3 years old, consider replacing them.

Prevention tip: Always charge after each full use. Never leave batteries discharged for extended periods. BIOCCE machines include intelligent battery indicators so you know exactly when to charge.

4. Uneven Cleaning Results

Cause: Worn or incorrect brush type, uneven brush pressure, dirty brushes, or inconsistent cleaning solution distribution.

Fix:

  • Inspect brushes for wear. Replace them when bristles are shorter than 15 mm.
  • Ensure the brush deck is level. An uneven deck applies uneven pressure.
  • Check that the correct brush type is installed for your floor surface (hard nylon for concrete, soft pad for tile).
  • Clean brushes thoroughly. Debris trapped in bristles reduces cleaning effectiveness.

Prevention tip: Rotate brushes every 3 months to ensure even wear. BIOCCE BC500 and BC600 walk-behind models use tool-free brush changes for quick maintenance.

5. Machine Shuts Down Unexpectedly

Cause: Low battery voltage, thermal overload protection triggered, blown fuse, or loose battery connections.

Fix:

  • Check the battery charge level. If the indicator shows low, recharge immediately.
  • Allow the machine to cool down if the thermal overload has tripped (common after extended use in hot environments).
  • Inspect fuses and circuit breakers. Replace any blown fuses with the correct rating.
  • Tighten battery terminals if connections are loose or corroded.

Prevention tip: BIOCCE ride-on scrubbers feature thermal protection and low-voltage cut-off systems that prevent deep discharge damage.

6. Brush or Pad Not Spinning

Cause: Broken drive belt, motor coupling failure, debris wrapped around the brush hub, or electrical connection issue.

Fix:

  • Turn off the machine and inspect the brush area for debris (string, wire, plastic wrap) that may be blocking rotation.
  • Check the drive belt. A broken belt requires replacement.
  • Test the brush motor by engaging the brush switch and listening for motor sound. No sound indicates an electrical issue.

Prevention tip: Inspect the brush area before each use and remove any debris from the floor. BIOCCE walk-behind scrubbers have an easy-access brush deck for quick inspection.

7. Excessive Noise or Vibration

Cause: Debris stuck in brush or squeegee assembly, worn bearings, loose components, or unbalanced brush.

Fix:

  • Stop the machine immediately and inspect for visible debris around rotating parts.
  • Check that the brush is properly seated and balanced.
  • Tighten any loose bolts or panel fasteners.
  • If noise persists, bearings may need lubrication or replacement. Consult a technician.

Prevention tip: Daily visual inspection takes 2 minutes and catches most issues before they cause noise or vibration. Follow the complete floor scrubber maintenance checklist for more detailed daily routines.

Quick Troubleshooting Checklist

When your floor scrubber is not working properly, run through this checklist before calling for service:

  1. Check the squeegee — Worn blades are the #1 cause of poor water pickup and streaking.
  2. Inspect the vacuum hose — Blockages here reduce suction dramatically.
  3. Clean the recovery tank — A dirty tank and float valve cause suction loss.
  4. Verify battery charge — Low battery causes weak brush motor and vacuum performance.
  5. Examine brushes — Worn or incorrect brushes affect cleaning quality.
  6. Check water flow settings — Too much or too little water causes poor results.
  7. Listen for unusual sounds — Early detection of noise prevents major repairs.

When to Call a Professional

  • Motor failure or burning smell from electrical components
  • Hydraulic system leaks (on ride-on models)
  • Structural damage to the chassis or tank
  • Persistent issues after applying all troubleshooting steps
