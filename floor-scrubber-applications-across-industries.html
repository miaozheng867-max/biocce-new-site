Floor Scrubber Applications Across Industries

How different sectors use floor scrubbers to maintain clean, safe facilities

Floor scrubbers are used across virtually every industry. The type of machine, cleaning frequency, and specific requirements vary significantly by sector.

Industry Applications

Warehousing and Logistics

Needs: Large open areas, 24/7 operations, dust and dirt from trucks and pallets

Best machines: Large ride-on scrubbers with lithium batteries for multi-shift use. Cylindrical brushes for grooved concrete floors. 50+ gallon tanks minimize refill stops.

Food and Beverage Processing

Needs: Sanitary conditions, grease and protein removal, washdown environments

Best machines: Stainless steel construction, hot water capable (180°F+), HEPA filtration, dedicated machines per zone to prevent cross-contamination.

Healthcare and Hospitals

Needs: Infection control, quiet operation during patient hours, frequent disinfection

Best machines: Compact walk-behinds with antimicrobial components, low-noise operation (under 65 dB), chemical injection systems for disinfectant application.

Retail and Hospitality

Needs: Aesthetic appearance, minimal disruption to customers, varied floor surfaces

Best machines: Walk-behinds with pad drivers for polished floors, compact design for navigating aisles and furniture, low-moisture systems for fast drying.

Manufacturing and Industrial

Needs: Heavy-duty cleaning, oil/grease removal, large facilities

Best machines: Industrial ride-on scrubbers with heavy brush pressure, debris collection systems, and durable components that withstand harsh environments.
